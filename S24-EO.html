Upholding the Democracy - My Summer with Elections Ontario. Introduction From April to August, 2024, I had the honour of joining a one of a kind organization whose main business objective is to facilitate elections in Ontario which are, quite literally the backbone of our society. My main reflections from this internship that I will bring into my future ambitions in interdisciplinary technical problem solving are:

1. Understanding cross-functional collaboration and integrated business systems and operations (government).
2. The value of human-centered, data-driven product design.
3. Applying strategies for analyzing future risk preparedness.
4. Full-time office etiquette.
5. Microsoft products such as Excel as database management tools.

In this report, I will reflect on my comprehensive experience, regarding my technical and cerebral professional development. Overall, I can successfully say I supported initiatives for improving Data and Social governance, learned a lot about enhancing electoral processes, as well as professionalism in the workplace.
The Office of the Chief Electoral Officer (Elections Ontario) About the employer Elections Ontario (EO) is an independent, nonpartisan office of the Legislative Assembly of Ontario, responsible for administering provincial elections, by-elections, and referenda in Ontario. We are committed to making voting easy and accessible for all electors, while maintaining the integrity, security, and transparency of the electoral process. Since Ontario’s first election in 1867, to the first official office of the Chief Electoral Officer in 1920, the organization has worked to facilitate Ontario’s democratic process.

Today, EO is an industry leader among Electoral Management Bodies (EMB’s) globally, and is constantly striving to maintain a balance of new ideas & innovations along with maintaining the core principles of democracy.

Everyone is a stakeholder for us, everyone is impacted by what we do! - Greg Essensa (CEO), Summer 2024 Intern Orientation

In the past 10 years, EO has made many advancements in technology including:
  • 2016: The Ontario Register Database of Future Voters (ages 16-17)
  • 2017: eRegistration, an online tool to simplify the registration process for Ontario voters.
  • 2018: Voter Information Service, providing voters easy digital access to their voting information.
  • 2018: 1st major technological advancements in 100 years, ePoll books and vote tabulators implemented across the province.
  • Present: Centralized intergovernmental databases of managing voter information
    • My Team: The Provincial Registry of Electors My Role in the company (job description) The goals of the Register Division are to maintain the integrity and accuracy of Ontario’s voter registration lists. This is done through consolidating bulk data from external suppliers and internal sources, then standardizing and transforming databases via data quality control checks to validate and apply corrections while ensuring data integrity, confidentiality, and security.

    My role as the Information Management Analyst Intern encompassed a variety of data-oriented tasks and projects enhancing register-related applications and database management systems. The specific computing domains covered are the intersection of Data Science, Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and their application to the digital humanities (social sciences). I mainly worked on various data warehouse centralization strategies, creating data pipelines for various address and property records to ensure accuracy within the registry list of electors.
